Website

I collaborated with my team for Wojciechowska Real Estate, a Poland-based company, to conceive and execute a new website. Our project involved the utilization of WordPress as our development framework, employing the Twenty Twenty-Three Theme in conjunction with custom HTML coding to craft an aesthetically pleasing and intuitive website. We integrated bespoke style elements, encompassing typography, color schemes, button design, and spacing, to harmonize with the company's branding. We also designed template parts to facilitate effortless page creation and reusability.

Process

Challenges

One of the trickiest parts of the project was making the filters work. We tried a few methods, like using special plugins called WooCommerce and Filter Everything - WooCommerce Product & WordPress Filter. These plugins would have allowed us to create fancy filters for our product listings with custom tags and categories. But, here's the hitch: our chosen theme, Twenty Twenty-Three, didn't support the kind of tools we needed for the filters to work. So, in the end, we had to manually use HTML and CSS to show how the filters would look on the page.

Result

The project culminated in the successful launch of Wojciechowska Real Estate's visually appealing and user-friendly website prototype. We implemented custom styles, including typography, color schemes, buttons, and spacing, to align with the company's branding. Template parts were designed for easy page creation and reuse. Despite challenges with filtering, we effectively showcased the intended system using HTML and CSS, ensuring the client's vision was communicated effectively.
